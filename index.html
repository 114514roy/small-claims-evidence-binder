Small Claims Evidence Binder

A local-first iOS app concept for turning photos, receipts, messages, estimates, and demand-letter records into a numbered exhibit packet before a dispute deadline.

Validation status: probe only. This page is testing demand for a local evidence-organization app before the app is built.

What It Would Do

Numbered exhibit index

List every photo, receipt, message screenshot, estimate, letter, and payment record with a clean exhibit number.

Timeline linked to evidence

Connect agreement, payment, damage, repair, demand-letter, and follow-up dates to the records that support them.

Missing-proof checklist

Flag source file names, missing dates, duplicate exhibit numbers, unlinked evidence, and exchange-note gaps.

Local export preview

Prepare a local Markdown/CSV packet preview first; full PDF or ZIP export would be the paid unlock if demand is proven.

Who This Is For

  • Renters preparing security-deposit evidence.
  • Freelancers or small contractors documenting unpaid invoices.
  • Homeowners organizing repair, warranty, or property-damage disputes.
  • Self-represented users who already have records and need them organized before a hearing, exchange deadline, mediation, or demand-letter follow-up.

Important Boundary

This concept is a local organization tool. It does not provide legal advice, court filing, court upload, certified records, deadline calculation, damages calculation, attorney review, or outcome guarantees.

Checklist Preview

  1. Write the dispute type, deadline, amount at stake, and what outcome you are asking for.
  2. Create one timeline row for each agreement, payment, incident, repair, message, estimate, demand letter, mailing record, and follow-up.
  3. Give every photo, receipt, message screenshot, estimate, and letter a unique exhibit number.
  4. Connect each timeline event to at least one exhibit, or mark the proof as missing.
  5. Check for missing source file names, dates, page counts, duplicate exhibit numbers, and unclear sender or recipient notes.
  6. Prepare a simple export packet with exhibit index, timeline, missing-proof checklist, and source-file list.
